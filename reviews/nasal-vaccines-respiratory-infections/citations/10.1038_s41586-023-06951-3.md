by which IT boosting resulted in robust and sustained enhancement of mucosal immunity and 185 protective efficacy (Extended Data Fig. 10). 186 187 **Discussion** ARTICLE 188 Our data demonstrate that Ad26 IT boosting robustly augmented mucosal humoral and 189 cellular immune responses and provided near complete protection against high-dose mucosal 190 SARS-CoV-2 BQ.1.1 challenge in rhesus macaques. Ad26 IT boosting induced greater mucosal 191 immunity than did Ad26 IN and IM boosting for all immunologic parameters evaluated. In 192 contrast, mRNA IN boosting proved ineffective, suggesting that improved formulations will 193 likely be required for effective mucosal delivery of mRNA vaccines. Of note, this mRNA 194 vaccine was highly immunogenic in macaques when delivered by the IM route29. Mucosal 195 humoral and cellular immune responses in BAL were the strongest immunologic correlates of 196 protection against mucosal SARS-CoV-2 challenge. Taken together, these data demonstrate that 197 novel immunization strategies can markedly increase mucosal immunity in nonhuman primates 198 and improve protective efficacy against a mucosal respiratory virus challenge. 199 Previous studies of mucosal immunization with SARS-CoV-2 vaccines have largely 200 focused on IN boosting strategies and have reported inconsistent increases in mucosal 201 immunity 3-7 . Additional studies have shown that inhalational delivery was superior to IN 202 delivery of an Ad5-TB vaccine in mice30 and that aerosol delivery was superior to IM delivery of 203 an Ad5-TB vaccine for induction of mucosal immune responses in humans31. An RSV vaccine 204 has also been shown to be superior when delivered by the IT route compared with the IN route in 205 baboons32. Moreover, a pharmacokinetic study in mice showed that IN administration of labeled 206 nanoparticles resulted in only 28% reaching the lung with substantial variability and most instead

# PREVIEW

reaching the stomach, whereas IT administration of nanoparticles resulted in 85-95% reaching 208 the lung, providing a potential explanation for the lack of consistency of IN boosting 209 approaches33. Our data confirm and extend these prior observations by demonstrating that Ad26 210 IT boosting was substantially more potent than Ad26 IN boosting for nearly all immunologic ARTICLE 211 parameters tested. Moreover, we show that Ad26 IT boosting provided near complete protection 212 against acquisition of infection following high-dose SARS-CoV-2 BQ.1.1 challenge in primates, 213 which overcomes a key limitation of current SARS-CoV-2 vaccines that do not appear to induce 214 robust mucosal immunity and do not protect against infection with current Omicron variants. 215 Vaccine delivery to the lung in humans can be performed by inhalation and nebulizer 216 technologies31. The CanSino Ad5 vaccine has been approved in China by the inhalational 217 route 34,35 , and the Bharat Biotech ChAd vaccine has been approved in India by the intranasal 218 route, thus demonstrating the clinical translatability of our findings. Our data comparing IN and 219 IT immunization extend these observations and suggest that delivery of the vaccine to the lung is 220 more effective than delivery of the vaccine to the nose. Moreover, our transcriptomics and 221 cytokine data suggest that the mechanism of IT boosting involves robust and sustained activation 222 of cytokine, NK, T and B cell pathways in the lung. 223 The inability of the bivalent SARS-CoV-2 mRNA vaccines delivered by the IM route to 224 provide robust protection against infection 1,2 likely relates to their inability to induce robust 225 mucosal immune responses at the portal of entry 10,11 . Our data demonstrate the proof-of-concept 226 that mucosal boosting by the IT route results in robust mucosal humoral and cellular immune 227 responses and near complete protection against SARS-CoV-2 Omicron challenge in macaques. 228 To the best of our knowledge, this degree of vaccine protection against SARS-CoV-2 Omicron 229 challenge in macaques is qualitatively different from what has been reported previously with IM
