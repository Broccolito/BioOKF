**Abstract**

The International Union of Immunological Societies (IUIS) expert committee (EC) on Inborn Errors

of Immunity (IEI) report here the 2022 updated phenotypic classification, which accompanies and

complements their genotypic classification.

This phenotypic classification is aimed for clinicians at the bedside and focuses on clinical features

and laboratory phenotypes. In this classification,485 IEI underlying phenotypes as diverse as infection,

malignancy, allergy, auto-immunity, and auto-inflammation, are described, including 55 novel

monogenic defects and 1 phenocopy due to autoantibodies. Therefore, all 485 diseases of the genetic

classification are presented in this paper in the form of colored tables with essentially clinical or

immunological phenotype entries.

**Introduction**

Human Inborn Errors of Immunity (IEI) comprise a large group of disorders presenting damaging

germline variants in the immune pathway. They can be dominant or recessive, autosomal or X-linked,

and with complete or incomplete penetrance. Clinical features are dominated by increased

susceptibility to a broad or narrow spectrum of infectious diseases, as well as a growing diversity of

autoimmune, autoinflammatory, allergic and/or malignant phenotypes. Based on stringent criteria

applied by the International Union of Immunological Societies (IUIS) expert committee, 55 novel

monogenic defects and 1 phenocopy have been added in the classification, bringing out the total

number of genetic defects to 485[1]. If most IEI are individually rare, they are collectively more

common than generally thought [2].

Since 2013, the (IUIS) expert committee on Primary Immunodeficiencies PID (IEI) proposes every

other year a phenotypic classification of all these disorders, in parallel with a genotypic catalog which

facilitates both research on, and diagnosis of, these conditions worldwide [1]. The phenotypic

classification is organized in diagnosis algorithms, based on the clinical and laboratory features

observed in these patients. This phenotypic classification is also adapted for smartphone applications

[3].

Here, we report an update of the phenotypic classification of IEI, based on the 2022 IEI classical

classification [1]. This tree-based decision-making process is aimed to physicians, regardless of their

familiarity with IEI. Its purpose is to guide the physician towards the most probable diagnosis based

on the clinical and laboratory features of their patient.

**Methodology**

All disorders indexed in the 2022 update of the IUIS IEI classification [1] are included in phenotypic

algorithms assigned to each of the ten main groups of the classification, and the same color was used

for each group of similar conditions. Given the exponential number of diseases, several categories

have been splitin two or three sub-figures to be more informative.

Disease names are presented in red and genes in bolditalic. An asterisk is added to highlight extremely

rare disorders (less than 10 reported cases to date). However, the reader should keep in mind that some

genes have been very recently described and that true prevalence is unknown. A double asterisk is

added when only one case or one kindred has been reported to date. In these cases, it is difficult to

confirm than observed phenotype would be reproducible in other patients carrying the same defect, or

if it is an atypical presentation.
