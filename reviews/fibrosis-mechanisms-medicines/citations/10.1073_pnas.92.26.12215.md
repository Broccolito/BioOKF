Early-onset multifocal inflammation in the transforming growth factor 131-null mouse is lymphocyte mediated

(severe combined immunodeficient mice/cardiac hyperplasia)

ABSTRACT Transforming growth factor 31 (TGFj81)-null mice die from complications due to an early-onset multifocal inflammatory disorder. We show here that cardiac cells are hyperproliferative and that intercellular adhesion molecule 1 (ICAM-1) is elevated. To determine which phenotypes are pri- marily caused by a deficiency in TGF,B1 from those that are secondarytoinflammation,we appliedimmunosuppressive ther- apy and genetic combination with the severe combined immu- nodeficiency (SCID) mutation to inhibit the inflammatory re- sponse. Treatment with antibodies to the leukocyte function- associated antigen 1 doubled longevity, reduced inflammation, and delayed heart cell proliferation. TGFP1-null SCID mice displayed no inflammation or cardiac cell proliferation, survived to adulthood, and exhibited normal major histocompatibility complex II (MHC II) and ICAM-1 levels.TGF.81-nullpups born to a TGFI8tknull SCID mother presented no gross congenital heart defects, indicating that TGF,B1 alone does not play an essential role in heart development. These results indicate that lymphocytes are essential for the inflammatory response, car- diac cell proliferation, and elevated MHC II and ICAM-1 ex- pression, revealing a vital role forTGFJ31 in regulating lympho- cyte proliferation and activation, which contribute to the main- tenance of selftolerance.

Transforming growth factor ,13 (TGF,B1) is the prototypic mem- berofthe largerTGF,B superfamilyofproteins thataffectgrowth and differentiation of many cell types (1, 2). Numerous studies have shown TGF(31 to be a potent modulator of immune re- sponses (3) and mice lacking TGFf31 develop an early-onset multifocal inflammation culminating in death around 3 weeks of age (4, 5). Further analysis of these mice has revealed elevated levels of peripheral lymphocytes and immature neutrophils (4), increasedexpressionofmajorhistocompatibilitycomplex(MHC) molecules (6), enhanced adhesive properties of TGFf1-null leukocytes (7), increased numbers ofproliferating cellsinmutant spleens and lymph nodes, and up-regulated thymic interleukin 2 expression (8). Since the onset of inflammation is detectable within the firstweek oflife (9) itisunclearwhether the deficiency in TGF,B1 or the inflammatory cells and their cytokines are the primary cause of the mutant phenotypes. To more accurately assess the in vivo functions ofTGFf31, we sought to suppress the chronic inflammation that invariably develops in these animals.

DISCUSSION

The immunodeficient TGFo1-null mice reveal an essential role for lymphocytes in the early-onset inflammation that invariably occurs in the immunocompetent mutants. Further, these results indicate a vital in vivo function for TGFf31 in regulating lymphocytic activation and proliferation, which contribute to the maintenance of self tolerance. Although immunodeficient TGFo31-null mice survive into adulthood, they are routinely50-80% the size of their TGFo1-expressing SCID littermates and do not thrive. This general lack ofvigor implicates other important physiological homeostatic func- tions for TGFf31. Nonetheless, immunodeficient TGFo1-null mice will be useful for reconstituting the inflammatory phe- notype and for analyzing the molecular and cellular effector mechanisms giving rise to widespread inflammation in the absence of TGFI31. The elevated numbers ofcirculatingleukocytes reported here, along with the histopathologic analysis of the heart, suggest that anti-CD11a therapy inhibits the extravasation of inflammatory cells that are, nonetheless, proliferating in the TGF(31-null mu- tants. The anti- CDlla-treated mutants also indicate that the enhanced cellular proliferation observed in the heart and blood of the TGFf31 mutants is a consequence of inflammation. This proliferative response likely results, in part, from the production of cytokines synthesized by TGFf31-null lymphocytes and/or by an increased sensitivity to such factors by peripheral tissues lacking TGF,B1. Some obvious candidate cytokines whose activities and expres- sion are modulated by TGF,B1, and which are elevated in the TGFl3-null mice, include tumor necrosis factor a, interferon Y, and interleukin 13 (4). Proinflammatory cytokines could also contribute to the elevated expression of cell adhesion (16) and MHC molecules (17). The observations described here indicate that the levels ofMHC II and ICAM-1 are not elevated in inflammation-free hearts of adult immunodeficient TGF31-null mice, although they undoubtedly contribute to the progressive pathology observed in the immunocompetent mutants. Contrary to a previous report (18), we find no gross abnor- malities in TGFf31-null pups born to a TGF,B1-null immunodefi- cient female. In the earlier study the mother had been kept alive by dexamethasome treatment which diminished, but did not eliminate, tissue inflammation similar to what is observed with anti-CD11a treatment. This suggests that inflammation and/or steroid treatment during pregnancy may have contributed to the congenital heart defects observed in the TGFl3-null pups. Just as TGFl3 modulates the inflammatory effectson cardiacgrowth in adults, so may itcarry out a similar function in utero to protect thefetusfrom chronicinflammation in the mother. Thus itwould appear that, at least on the strainC3H background,TGF,B1 isnot essential for heart development.
