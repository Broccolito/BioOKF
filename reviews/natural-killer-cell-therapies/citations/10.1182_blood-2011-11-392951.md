**Cell sorting experiments**

PBMCs were washed with FACS buffer and incubated with 10 g/mL human IgG before surface staining to block nonspecific antibody binding. After surface staining, the PBMCs were stained with PE-conjugated anti–Tim-3, FITC-conjugated anti-CD94, PE-Cy7–conjugated anti-CD56, Pacific blue-conjugated anti-CD16, ECD-conjugated anti-CD3, APC-Cy7– conjugated anti-CD14, and APC-Cy7–conjugated anti-CD19; the cells were sorted using a modified BD FACSAria (BD Biosciences). To isolate NK cells, we sorted CD19, CD14, CD3, CD56dimCD16,or CD56brightCD16 either lacking or expressing Tim-3 on their surface.

**Expression of Tim-3 and NK-cell activity**

We have shown previously that increased amounts of Tim-3 on 19 T cells renders them dysfunctional. Therefore, we examined whether the amount of Tim-3 on the cell surface of NK cells correlated with their functional responses. CD56brightCD16–NK cells are the predominant NK-cell subset secreting IFN-in response to IL-12 IL-18.29Indeed, a higher percentage of the CD56brightNK-cell subset produced IFN-compared with the CD56dimNK cells in response to activation with these cytokines, although a significant proportion of CD56dimNK cells also produced IFN-in response to IL-12 IL-18 (Figure 3A-B). Most of the CD56brightNK cells that produced IFN-in response to IL-12 IL-18 stimulation were Tim-3 cells (Figure 2A-B) and displayed a high cell surface density of Tim-3 (Figure 3C). The dim CD56 NK cells that produced IFN-in response to IL- 12 IL-18 stimulation also expressed the highest cell surface density of Tim-3 (Figure 3A-C). The MFI of Tim-3 staining was dim higher on CD56 NK cells producing IFN-(MFI median, 3110; IQR, 2903, 3693) compared with the IFN-–negative NK cells (MFI median, 2240; IQR, 1404, 2532; P.004; Figure 3C). Interestingly, the CD56 CD16 NK cells degranulating expressed the highest surface bright density – of Tim-3, suggesting that the more mature CD56 CD16 Tim-3 NK cells were the cells degranulating when cocultured with target cells (Figure 4C). Tim-3 cell surface dim expression was similar between CD56 CD16 NK cells degranu- lating or not (Figure 4C).
