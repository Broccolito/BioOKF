## Entity & relation patterns observed

This source class (chemistry / chemical biology / medicinal chemistry) is **compound-centric**: nearly every item revolves around a small molecule and the biological macromolecule(s) it acts on, plus the experimental measurement and synthetic provenance that connect them. Compared with clinical or genomics sources, the distinctive contribution to the BioOKF type universe is a rich layer of **chemical-structure entities** and **quantitative bioactivity / chemical-transformation edges**.

### Recurring entity (node) types

- **Small molecule / Compound** — the dominant node. Sub-flavors: drug, clinical candidate, lead, hit, fragment, chemical probe, natural product, metabolite, prodrug, payload, covalent warhead, molecular glue, PROTAC (bifunctional), ligand, scaffold/chemotype. Carries structure identifiers: SMILES, InChI, ChEMBL ID, PubChem CID/SID, molecular formula.
- **Chemical substructure / moiety** — scaffold, pharmacophore, warhead, zinc-binding group, linker, bioisostere, functional group, ring system, R-group. Important because SAR papers operate at the substructure level.
- **Protein target** — enzymes (kinases, proteases, transglutaminase, HDAC, PARP, aromatase, transferases), receptors (GPCRs, nuclear receptors), epigenetic readers (bromodomains), E3 ubiquitin ligases (CRBN, VHL), transporters, protein–protein-interaction interfaces.
- **Binding site / residue** — pocket, active site, allosteric site, catalytic cysteine/lysine (for covalent chemistry), induced pocket.
- **Assay / measurement** — biochemical inhibition assay, binding assay, FRET, SPR, NMR, X-ray co-crystal, cell-based assay, MIC determination, BioAssay (AID).
- **Quantitative property** — IC50, Ki, Kd, EC50, k_inact/K_I, MIC, DC50/Dmax, ligand efficiency, ee, pKa, logP, ADMET endpoints.
- **Reaction / synthetic transformation** — reaction (reactant→product), reagent, catalyst, reaction conditions, mechanism (radical, SET, enamine/iminium activation).
- **Organism / biological context** — source organism (for natural products), pathogen (bacterium, virus, parasite) for anti-infective potency, cell line.
- **Disease / indication** — cancer (subtypes), infection (COVID-19, TB, malaria), celiac disease, fibrosis, CNS/psychiatric disorders.
- **Pathway / biological process** — ubiquitin–proteasome system, signal-transduction cascade, epigenetic signaling, metabolic pathway.
- **Data resource / database** — ChEMBL, PubChem, BindingDB, DrugBank, PDBe/CCD, Open Reaction Database (as provenance nodes).

### Recurring relationship (edge) types

- **INHIBITS / MODULATES / ACTIVATES** — compound → protein target (with directionality: agonist/antagonist, positive/negative allosteric modulator, activator/inhibitor).
- **BINDS / has affinity (quantified)** — compound → target, annotated with assay type and a numeric value (IC50/Ki/Kd/EC50). This is the highest-value, most frequent edge in the class.
- **COVALENTLY MODIFIES** — warhead-bearing compound → specific residue (e.g., catalytic Cys); irreversible/reversible covalent qualifier.
- **DEGRADES / RECRUITS** — PROTAC or molecular glue → (E3 ligase + protein-of-interest) → ubiquitination → proteasomal degradation; ternary-complex relation.
- **HAS SUBSTRUCTURE / SHARES SCAFFOLD / IS BIOISOSTERE OF** — compound ↔ moiety/scaffold; underlies SAR and scaffold-hopping.
- **STRUCTURE–ACTIVITY (modification → potency change)** — chemical change on a scaffold → measured shift in activity/selectivity; the defining relation of medicinal chemistry.
- **IS SELECTIVE FOR / OVER** — compound → target A vs. off-target B (selectivity ratio).
- **TRANSFORMS INTO (reaction)** — reactant → product, mediated by catalyst/reagent under conditions; carries mechanism and yield.
- **CATALYZES** — catalyst/enzyme → reaction (and enantioselectivity for asymmetric catalysis).
- **ISOLATED FROM / DERIVED FROM** — natural product → source organism; analog → parent compound.
- **TESTED IN (assay)** — compound → assay → readout, with active/inactive or quantitative outcome; links to organism/cell line/pathogen.
- **TREATS / INDICATED FOR** — drug/compound → disease/indication.
- **TARGETS PATHWAY / PARTICIPATES IN** — compound or target → biological pathway/process.
- **PREDICTED (computational)** — compound → predicted target / predicted ADMET property / predicted binding pose (model-derived edges, distinct from measured ones — useful to flag provenance/confidence).
- **CO-CRYSTALLIZED WITH / has binding pose in** — compound → protein structure (PDB), with binding-mode geometry.

### Modeling notes for BioOKF

1. **Quantitative edges need attributes.** A bare "compound–inhibits–target" edge loses most of the value; the class demands edge properties for value, unit, assay type, and measurement context (target organism/construct).
2. **Measured vs. predicted provenance.** Computational (docking/QSAR/target-prediction) sources and experimental sources assert the *same* edge type at very different confidence — provenance/confidence should be first-class.
3. **Substructure granularity.** SAR, fragment, covalent, and TPD sources operate below the whole-molecule level (scaffold, warhead, linker, ZBG, E3-ligand handle); a Compound–hasMoiety–Substructure layer is needed to capture them.
4. **Ternary / multi-body relations.** PROTACs and molecular glues are inherently 3-body (compound + E3 ligase + POI); n-ary or reified relations beat simple binary edges here.
5. **Reaction edges are directional and conditioned.** Synthesis/mechanism sources contribute reactant→product edges qualified by catalyst, reagent, and conditions — a different edge family from bioactivity, but co-occurring in the same corpus.
6. **Databases are both sources and node namespaces.** ChEMBL/PubChem/BindingDB/PDBe IDs should anchor compound and target identity for cross-source entity resolution.
