# Epidemiology & Clinical Science — Source Catalog

A catalog of real, openly-accessible peer-reviewed epidemiology and clinical
science research articles — cohort, case-control, RCT, meta-analysis,
cross-sectional, Mendelian randomization, and surveillance/outbreak studies
spanning cardiovascular/metabolic, infectious disease, cancer, nutrition/lifestyle/
public health, neurology/mental health/maternal-child, and pharmacoepidemiology/
methods/health-services subfields. Compiled for the BioOKF type universe: each row
notes the biomedical **entities** (exposures, outcomes, populations/cohorts,
interventions, risk factors) and the **relationships** (statistical associations:
OR, HR, RR, VE, 95% CI, p-value) the source reports.

All URLs were located via web search and each article page was fetched and its
title confirmed during compilation (2026-06-25). PMC = PubMed Central open-access
full text; PLOS = Public Library of Science (CC-BY). Most HTML article pages also
expose a PDF download. `www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/...` 301-redirects to the canonical
`pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/...` host used below.

## Entity & relation patterns observed

Epidemiology and clinical-science sources are unusually regular in structure: nearly
every article instantiates the same core schema — **a population exposed to something,
followed for an outcome, with the exposure→outcome link quantified by an effect
measure with uncertainty bounds.** This makes them a high-signal corpus for knowledge-graph
extraction. The recurring node and edge types observed across all 104 items:

### Entity (node) types

- **Populations / cohorts / study arms** — named cohorts (Framingham, ARIC, MESA, CARDIA,
  EPIC-InterAct, NHANES, UK Biobank, NIH-AARP, SEER), trial arms (intervention vs placebo/control),
  case vs control groups, biobanks, and surveillance populations. Often carry attributes:
  sample size (n), age range, sex, ethnicity, country, follow-up duration.
- **Exposures / risk factors** — behavioral (smoking, alcohol, diet, physical activity, sitting,
  sleep), physiologic/biomarker (BMI, LDL-C, HbA1c, blood pressure, hsCRP, coronary artery calcium),
  environmental/occupational (PM2.5 air pollution, pesticides, secondhand smoke), infectious
  (SARS-CoV-2, EBV, HIV, M. tuberculosis, HPV, Vibrio cholerae), social (social determinants of
  health), and genetic (APOE ε4, polygenic scores, SNP instruments).
- **Outcomes / diseases / endpoints** — incident disease (T2D, hypertension, CVD, MI, stroke,
  dementia, Parkinson's, MS, cancer by site), mortality (all-cause, cause-specific, CV, cancer),
  surrogate/clinical endpoints (mRS, MADRS, disease-free/recurrence-free/overall survival,
  SVR12, viral suppression), and adverse events.
- **Interventions / treatments** — drugs (statins, metformin, dexamethasone, alteplase,
  pembrolizumab, FOLFOX, antidepressants, ketamine, NSAIDs, DAAs, ACTs), vaccines (BNT162b2,
  RTS,S, BCG, HPV, influenza), procedures (thrombectomy, screening colonoscopy/mammography/PSA/FIT,
  LDCT), and lifestyle/policy interventions (DASH diet, PrEP, SSB tax).
- **Study designs** — a meta-entity attached to nearly every article: cohort (prospective/retrospective),
  case-control (incl. nested and test-negative), case-cohort, RCT (incl. cluster/community-randomized,
  open-label), meta-analysis / systematic review, cross-sectional, Mendelian randomization,
  target-trial emulation, surveillance/outbreak investigation, ecological/quasi-experimental.
- **Effect measures / statistics** — odds ratio (OR), hazard ratio (HR), relative risk / risk
  ratio (RR), rate ratio / incidence rate ratio (IRR), mortality rate ratio (MRR), vaccine
  efficacy/effectiveness (VE), standardized mean difference, reproduction number (Rt), each
  typically paired with a 95% confidence interval, a p-value, and sometimes NNT/NNS or
  c-statistic/AUC for prediction models.

### Relationship (edge) types

- **EXPOSURE → OUTCOME (association, quantified)** — the dominant edge. Reified with the effect
  measure as an edge attribute: e.g. *smoking —[OR 103.5]→ lung cancer*, *BMI —[HR 2.76]→
  mortality*, *EBV —[32× hazard]→ MS*. Direction may be harmful (RR/HR/OR > 1) or protective (< 1).
- **INTERVENTION → OUTCOME (causal effect from RCT/trial emulation)** — *statin —[HR 0.69]→ CVD*,
  *BNT162b2 —[VE 95%]→ symptomatic COVID-19*, *dexamethasone —[RR 0.83]→ 28-day mortality*.
- **RISK FACTOR → RISK SCORE / PREDICTION** — predictors feeding a model that maps to outcome
  probability (Framingham, QRISK3, TRIPOD-style models); edge carries discrimination (c-statistic).
- **GENETIC VARIANT → INTERMEDIATE PHENOTYPE → DISEASE (causal, Mendelian randomization)** — a
  three-node causal chain using SNPs as instruments: *SNP —→ LDL-C —[OR 1.49]→ CHD*.
- **STUDY ⟶ POPULATION ⟶ EXPOSURE/OUTCOME (provenance/measurement)** — every association is
  scoped to a study, a cohort, a sample size, and a follow-up window — context edges essential
  for evidence weighting.
- **DRUG → ADVERSE EVENT (pharmacovigilance)** — *NSAID —[OR ~1.5]→ acute MI*, *gabapentin+opioid
  —[OR 1.49]→ opioid-related death*.
- **EXPOSURE ⊣ CONFOUNDER / BIAS (methodologic)** — methods papers add edges describing how
  confounding, immortal-time bias, and selection distort the exposure→outcome estimate (DAGs,
  propensity scores, target-trial emulation).

### Modifiers and qualifiers worth capturing

Effect estimates are rarely unconditional. Recurring qualifiers that belong on edges: **dose-response**
(per serving/day, per 10 µg/m³, per 1 mmol/L, per SD), **time-windowed** (years since quitting,
days of drug use, follow-up duration), **subgroup/effect-modification** (by age, sex, ethnicity,
PD-L1 status, hormone-receptor status, baseline severity), **adjustment status** (crude vs
adjusted/multivariable), and **directionality/significance** (CI crossing 1.0, p-value threshold).
A faithful extraction treats the association itself as a first-class, attribute-rich edge rather
than a simple binary link.
