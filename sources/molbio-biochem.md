### Implications for BioOKF

The class strongly favors a **directed, signed, typed property graph**. Most edges are
directional and carry sign (activate vs inhibit) and mechanism (phosphorylation,
ubiquitination, allostery), so edge types should be *mechanism-qualified* rather than a
generic "interacts." Reactions/events and pathways behave as first-class nodes (Reactome
and KEGG model them explicitly), arguing for an event/process node type plus
participant-role edges (catalyst, input, output, regulator). Proteins need rich sub-entity
structure (domain / site / residue / PTM) to anchor mechanistic edges precisely, and a
Geneâ†’Proteinâ†’Structure(PDB)â†’Function chain recurs across nearly every source. Finally, the
literature consistently terminates pathway descriptions in **disease/phenotype** and
**drug** nodes, so those node types and the variantâ†’functionâ†’disease and drugâ†’target edges
are essential for the biomedical layer.
