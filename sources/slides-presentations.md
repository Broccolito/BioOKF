# Biomedical Slide Decks, Presentations & Posters Source Catalog (BioOKF)

Openly-accessible biomedical **conference posters, talk slides, and presentations** drawn
from research-output repositories: **Zenodo** (CERN open-access archive — posters &
presentations with DOIs), **Figshare** and its institutional instances (figshare.com,
epa.figshare.com, indigo.uic.edu, crick.figshare.com, sussex.figshare.com,
auckland.figshare.com, kilthub.cmu.edu, repository.rcsi.com, agresearch.figshare.com —
the host of most ISMB/ISCB and lab posters), and **SlideShare** (educational lecture
decks). Many of these double as the publication venue for **F1000Research / ISCB**
posters, which are deposited to figshare/Zenodo with a citable DOI.

This source class encodes knowledge as **bullet points + figures** rather than running
prose: a poster/slide states a handful of claims (often one per panel — a volcano plot, a
network diagram, a survival curve, a bar chart) tying a few biomedical entities together.
That makes them dense in **entity-relation triples but sparse in connecting narrative** -
the relation is frequently *implied by the figure type* (a Manhattan plot => variant-trait
association; a KEGG/network panel => gene/protein interaction; a Kaplan-Meier curve =>
treatment/marker-survival).

All records were verified live and public via the Zenodo and Figshare REST APIs (and
SlideShare via HTTP 200), June 2026. Repository record pages may return 403 to scripted
`curl` (anti-bot) but resolve normally in a browser. Most items resolve to a downloadable
**PDF** poster/slide; a few are PPTX, slide images, or HTML landing pages.

## Entity & relation patterns observed

Posters and slide decks are a **figure-anchored, claim-dense** source class. Unlike a journal
article (which carries a long argument with hedging and methods), a poster panel or a slide
typically asserts **one compact relationship** — and the relationship is very often *encoded by
the figure type itself*. This makes the class unusually valuable for harvesting clean
entity–relation triples, but it also means the connecting evidence/qualifiers live in the
surrounding bullets, the methods micro-print, or the figure caption rather than in prose. For
BioOKF, the distinctive contributions of this class are (a) a **broad, shallow** coverage of
almost every biomedical entity type (because a single conference samples all subfields at once),
and (b) a tight coupling between **figure archetype and edge type** that can be exploited for
extraction.

### Recurring entity (node) types

- **Gene / transcript / isoform** — the most frequent node; carried by RNA-seq, single-cell,
  GWAS, CRISPR, and cancer-genomics posters (e.g. RIN3, DMPK, lncRNAs, transcript isoforms).
- **Variant / mutation** — SNPs, missense/germline variants, VUS, structural variants, eccDNA
  amplifications; the unit of GWAS, clinical-genomics, and variant-effect-prediction posters.
- **Protein / peptide / protein complex** — proteomics and structural posters: interactomes,
  human protein-complex atlases (hu.MAP), surfaceomes, antibody–antigen, AlphaFold/ESM structures.
- **Cell type / cell state** — single-cell and spatial-omics posters are organized around cell
  clusters/states (T cells, microglia, hematopoietic states, tumor-microenvironment cells).
- **Tissue / organ / anatomical region** — brain regions, lung, spinal cord lamina, tumor
  microenvironment; the spatial context for expression and disease.
- **Disease / phenotype / condition** — cancer (sub)types, Alzheimer's, myotonic dystrophy,
  diabetes, endometriosis, myeloproliferative neoplasms, Q fever, COVID-19.
- **Small molecule / compound / drug** — docking hits, inhibitors, natural products, CADD leads,
  checkpoint-blockade therapies, antisense oligonucleotides.
- **Metabolite** — metabolomics posters (NMR/MS), microbial metabolites, mannose metabolism.
- **Microbial taxon / strain / pathogen** — microbiome posters (gut taxa, Streptococcus,
  Candida auris), pathogen-genomics (SARS-CoV-2, M. tuberculosis, Shigella, hepatitis).
- **Pathway / biological process / network** — signaling (Wnt, PI3K, retinoic acid), immune
  response, metabolic pathways, gene/protein interaction networks.
- **Genomic region / locus / element** — GWAS loci, CpG sites, regulatory regions, breakpoints.
- **Patient / cohort / sample** — clinical and epidemiology posters (incidence/prevalence,
  eligibility, All of Us, biobanks); the population context.
- **Assay / measurement / readout** — expression quantification, docking score, survival
  endpoint, cytokine release, binding affinity, MIC.
- **Tool / database / pipeline / resource** — a large fraction of poster *content* is itself a
  software/data artifact (Galaxy/Snakemake pipelines, BacDive, iAtlas, GAMBL, Beacon, EMBL-EBI
  APIs); these act as both nodes and as **provenance/namespace anchors**.
- **Organism / model system** — model organisms, cell lines (U2OS, HepG2), xenografts, mouse
  models, humanized models.

### Recurring relationship (edge) types

- **EXPRESSED-IN / MARKER-OF** — gene/protein → cell type or tissue (single-cell, spatial).
- **DIFFERENTIALLY-EXPRESSED-BETWEEN** — gene/transcript → condition pair (RNA-seq volcano plots).
- **ASSOCIATED-WITH** — variant/SNP → trait/disease (GWAS Manhattan plots); also metabolite/
  taxon → phenotype. The dominant, figure-implied edge of the class.
- **MUTATED-IN / DRIVES** — gene/variant → cancer or disease (oncogenomics).
- **CLASSIFIED-AS / CAUSES** — variant → pathogenicity / Mendelian disease (clinical genomics).
- **INTERACTS-WITH / MEMBER-OF** — protein ↔ protein; protein → complex (interactomics, networks).
- **BINDS / INHIBITS / MODULATES** — compound → protein target; antibody → antigen; with
  affinity/docking-score attributes (drug discovery, structural).
- **EDITS / PERTURBS → READOUT** — CRISPR guide/screen → target gene → phenotypic readout.
- **REGULATES / METHYLATED-IN** — histone mark / CpG / regulatory element → gene (epigenomics).
- **PARTICIPATES-IN / PART-OF-PATHWAY** — gene/protein/metabolite → pathway or process.
- **INFECTS / CONFERS-RESISTANCE / BINDS-RECEPTOR** — pathogen → host; protein → host receptor
  (e.g. Spike–ACE2); variant → drug resistance or virulence (pathogen genomics).
- **PRODUCES / ABUNDANT-IN** — microbial taxon → metabolite; taxon → host/condition (microbiome).
- **TREATS / ELIGIBLE-FOR / INDICATED-FOR** — drug/therapy → disease; cohort → treatment eligibility.
- **HAS-INCIDENCE / PREVALENCE-IN** — disease → population/region (epidemiology posters).
- **AGGREGATES → / IMPLICATED-IN** — protein/mutation → neurodegeneration or disease mechanism.
- **PROCESSES / CATALOGS / PREDICTS** — tool/pipeline/model → entities and edges it produces
  (a *provenance* edge: many posters describe the method that *generates* the other edges).

### Modeling notes for BioOKF

1. **Figure archetype ⇒ edge type.** The strongest signal in this class is visual: a volcano
   plot ⇒ differential-expression edges; a Manhattan plot ⇒ variant–trait association; a
   network/KEGG panel ⇒ interaction/pathway edges; a Kaplan–Meier curve ⇒ marker/treatment–
   survival; a UMAP ⇒ cell-type/state membership; a docking pose ⇒ compound–target binding.
   An extractor should treat the **figure type as a prior on the relation type**.
2. **Claims are atomic but under-qualified.** Each panel asserts a triple, but the qualifiers
   (effect size, p-value, sample size, direction, organism, assay) live in micro-print, axis
   labels, or bullets. Edges harvested here need **attribute slots** and should carry a *lower
   default confidence* than peer-reviewed full text — posters are frequently **un-peer-reviewed,
   early-stage** (F1000/figshare posters are explicitly *not* peer reviewed).
3. **Provenance/method nodes are first-class.** A large share of items *are about a tool,
   pipeline, database, or model* rather than a biological finding. Modeling Tool/Resource as a
   node with PRODUCES/PROCESSES edges captures both the artifact and the method-provenance of
   the biological edges it generates.
4. **Breadth over depth — good for type-universe coverage, weak for resolution.** One conference
   poster session samples nearly every entity and edge type in the BioOKF universe, which makes
   this class ideal for *validating the type schema is complete*. But entity identifiers are
   often informal (a gene symbol, a strain name, a cell-line nickname) with no accession, so
   **entity resolution / grounding** is harder than in database-backed sources.
5. **Self-contained, multi-entity panels invite n-ary relations.** Many panels relate three or
   more entities at once (compound + target + disease; variant + gene + phenotype + cohort;
   taxon + metabolite + host condition). Reified/n-ary relations capture these better than
   chains of binary edges.
6. **Repository metadata is reliable scaffolding.** DOI, deposit date, authors, subject
   keywords, and institution come cleanly from the Zenodo/Figshare APIs even when the poster PDF
   itself is hard to parse — useful for dating, attribution, and coarse subfield tagging of every
   extracted edge.
