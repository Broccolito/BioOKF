# Tumour heterogeneity

Spatial and temporal genetic diversity within a tumour, generated by mutational processes (age-related
mutations, APOBEC editing, genomic instability, chromothripsis, chromosomal instability). It is
perhaps the most easily conceptualized cause of drug resistance: targeting an "actionable" driver is
only effective if the mutation is truncal (clonal). Subclonal driver mutations — e.g. in ESR1 or the
PI3K pathway — are sufficient to drive resistance, motivating a catalogue of driver "clonality."
