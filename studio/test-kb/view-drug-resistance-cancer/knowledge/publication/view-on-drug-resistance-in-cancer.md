# A view on drug resistance in cancer

Review article (Nature, 13 November 2019) by Neil Vasan, José Baselga and David M. Hyman.
It takes a reductionist approach to the biological determinants of drug resistance in cancer —
tumour burden and growth kinetics; tumour heterogeneity; physical barriers; the immune system
and microenvironment; undruggable cancer drivers; and the consequences of therapeutic pressure —
and proposes four general solutions: earlier detection (cancer interception), deeper responses,
adaptive therapeutic monitoring, and exploitation of cancer dependencies via synthetic-lethality
screens and computational modelling.
