# CRISPR-Cas9 synthetic lethality screen

Genome-scale CRISPR-Cas9 knockout screening identifies genes whose deletion confers sensitivity or
resistance to a given driver or drug. Relative to shRNA, CRISPR yields fewer off-target effects and
higher efficiency because a single-guide RNA can cleave both alleles. One of the first genome-wide
CRISPR knockout screens mapped mediators of resistance to RAF inhibition in BRAF-mutant melanoma,
and the approach now drives mapping of cancer dependencies and synthetic-lethal vulnerabilities,
including to immune checkpoint blockade.
