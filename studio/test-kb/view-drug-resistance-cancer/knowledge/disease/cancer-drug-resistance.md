# Cancer drug resistance

Drug resistance is the principal limiting factor to achieving cures in patients with cancer.
The review frames cancer therapy as a three-component system — a therapy that targets a population
of cancer cells within a particular host environment — and separates the biological determinants of
resistance: tumour burden and growth kinetics, tumour heterogeneity, physical barriers (sanctuary
sites, the blood-brain barrier), the immune system and microenvironment, undruggable drivers, and
the consequences of selective therapeutic pressure (adaptive responses and acquired resistance).
