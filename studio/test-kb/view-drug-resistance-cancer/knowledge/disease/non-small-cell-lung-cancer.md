# Non-small-cell lung cancer

NSCLC is a recurring example in the review. EGFR-mutant NSCLC initially responds to first-generation
EGFR tyrosine kinase inhibitors but develops acquired resistance, most commonly via the EGFR T790M
gatekeeper mutation, which is targeted by osimertinib. ALK-positive NSCLC responds to alectinib,
which prolongs progression-free survival versus first-generation ALK inhibitors. Treatment-induced
transformation of EGFR-mutant NSCLC to small-cell-like neuroendocrine phenotypes (with loss of RB1
and TP53) is a histological mechanism of resistance.
