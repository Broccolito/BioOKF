# Antifungal drug resistance

Antifungal drug resistance is the ability of fungi to survive drug concentrations that would
normally inhibit or kill them. The review frames the worldwide emergence of antifungal
resistance — driven partly by agricultural azole use — as a threat to both human health and
food security, made more dangerous by the small number of antifungal drug classes (azoles,
polyenes, echinocandins) available. Emerging pathogens such as *Candida auris* are often
multidrug resistant.
