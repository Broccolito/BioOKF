# Cryptococcus gattii

*Cryptococcus gattii* is an encapsulated yeast pathogen that causes cryptococcosis in
otherwise healthy hosts. The review cites it as an example of a fungus crossing into new
hosts and geographies: the VGIIa type infected harbor seals (*Phoca vitulina*) in British
Columbia, Canada, illustrating fungal threats to wildlife linked to environmental change.
