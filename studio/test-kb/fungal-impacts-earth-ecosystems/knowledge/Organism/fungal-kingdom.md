# Fungal kingdom

The fungal kingdom (Fungi) has diversified over the past billion years to more than two
million species, with over 95% still undescribed. Fungi are heterotrophs that feed on
almost any organic carbon, recycling nutrients through the decay of dead plants and animals
and sequestering carbon into Earth's ecosystems. Beyond macroscopic mushrooms and
microscopic yeast, the kingdom spans mutualists (mycorrhizae), saprotrophs and pathogens of
plants, wildlife and humans.
