# Climate change

Climate change is the long-term shift in temperature and weather patterns driven by human
activity. The review identifies it as a key amplifier of fungal threats: warming may select
for thermotolerant fungi able to infect endothermic hosts, precipitation/heat/drought drive
coccidioidomycosis expansion, and shifting climate disrupts fungal interactions in plants,
wildlife and humans (Fig. 1). It is also projected to substantially reduce ectomycorrhizal
fungal diversity in North American forests.
