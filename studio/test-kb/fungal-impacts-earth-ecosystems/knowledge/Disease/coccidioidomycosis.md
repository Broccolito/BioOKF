# Coccidioidomycosis

Coccidioidomycosis (Valley fever) is the disease caused by inhaling *Coccidioides* spores,
endemic to the arid western United States. The review highlights it as a climate-sensitive
fungal disease: a longitudinal surveillance study found that precipitation, heat and drought
drive the incidence and the geographic expansion of coccidioidomycosis, projecting further
spread under climate change.
