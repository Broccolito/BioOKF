# Antitumor immune response

The antitumor immune response is the host cytotoxic programme (natural killer and T cells) that
recognizes and eliminates transformed colonocytes. Tumour-associated microbiota subvert it:
*Fusobacterium nucleatum* Fap2 engages the inhibitory receptor TIGIT to suppress immune-cell
attack, helping colorectal tumours evade clearance (Gur et al., Immunity 2015, as reviewed by
Janney et al., Nature 2020).
