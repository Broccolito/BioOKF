# DNA double-strand breaks

DNA double-strand breaks (DSBs) are a severe form of DNA damage observed in colonic epithelial
cells exposed to microbial genotoxins. Colibactin from *pks*+ *E. coli* and secondary bile acids
induce DSBs and genomic instability in vivo, generating the mutational signatures that underlie
colorectal cancer driver mutations (Nougayrede et al., Science 2006; Cuevas-Ramos et al., PNAS
2010, as reviewed by Janney et al., Nature 2020).
