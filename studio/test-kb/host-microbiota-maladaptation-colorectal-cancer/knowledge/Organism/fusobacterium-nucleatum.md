# Fusobacterium nucleatum

*Fusobacterium nucleatum* is an oral commensal that is enriched in colorectal tumours, where it
potentiates intestinal tumorigenesis and modulates the tumour-immune microenvironment (Kostic et
al., Cell Host Microbe 2013). Its FadA adhesin engages host E-cadherin to activate Wnt/β-catenin
signalling, and its Fap2 lectin binds the inhibitory receptor TIGIT to shield tumours from immune
attack (Gur et al., Immunity 2015). *Fusobacterium* persists through CRC metastasis and antibiotic
treatment reduces tumour burden (Bullman et al., Science 2017, as reviewed by Janney et al.,
Nature 2020).
