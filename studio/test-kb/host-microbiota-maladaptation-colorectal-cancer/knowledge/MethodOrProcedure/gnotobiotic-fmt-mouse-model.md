# Gnotobiotic faecal transplant mouse model

Gnotobiotic (germ-free) mice colonized with defined or patient-derived microbiota are the central
experimental system for establishing causality in microbiota-driven colorectal cancer. Gavaging
faecal samples from patients with CRC into germ-free or conventional mice promotes intestinal
carcinogenesis, while gnotobiotic models also demonstrate fiber- and butyrate-dependent protection
(Wong et al., Gastroenterology 2017; Donohoe et al., Cancer Discov. 2014, as reviewed by Janney et
al., Nature 2020).
