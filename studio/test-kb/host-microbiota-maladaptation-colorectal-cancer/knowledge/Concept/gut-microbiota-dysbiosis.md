# Gut microbiota dysbiosis

Gut microbiota dysbiosis denotes the altered, 'oncogenic' CRC-associated microbial community
described across the literature. Faecal transplantation of stools from patients with CRC into
germ-free or conventional mice promotes intestinal carcinogenesis, illustrating a carcinogenic
role for this dysbiotic community; whether dysbiosis is causal or a bystander effect of
established tumours remains an open question (Wong et al., Gastroenterology 2017, as reviewed by
Janney et al., Nature 2020).
