# Butyrate

Butyrate is a short-chain fatty acid produced by commensal bacteria fermenting dietary fiber. It
inhibits histone deacetylases (HDAC), signals through the receptor GPR109A (the niacin receptor),
and shapes intestinal macrophage function, suppressing colonic inflammation and carcinogenesis. In
gnotobiotic models, dietary fiber protects against colorectal tumorigenesis in a microbiota- and
butyrate-dependent manner (Donohoe et al., Cancer Discov. 2014; Singh et al., Immunity 2014;
Chang et al., PNAS 2014, as reviewed by Janney et al., Nature 2020).
