# Dietary fiber

Dietary fiber is the fermentable plant carbohydrate that commensal bacteria convert into
short-chain fatty acids, principally butyrate. In gnotobiotic mouse models, dietary fiber protects
against colorectal tumorigenesis in a microbiota- and butyrate-dependent manner; conversely,
fiber deprivation shifts the microbiota toward mucus degradation and barrier breakdown (Donohoe et
al., Cancer Discov. 2014; Desai et al., Cell 2016, as reviewed by Janney et al., Nature 2020).
